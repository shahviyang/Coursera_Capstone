							<h1 class="entry-title">10 of the best Seattle-area parks for kids</h1>			<h3 class="entry-subtitle">Whether you're looking for a challenging hike, a walk in the woods or an awesome playground, here's where to go.</h3>			<div class="entry-meta">

			<p dir="ltr">Best Seattle parks for kids: Seattle is chock-full of gorgeous parks with something for every family. Whether you&#8217;re looking for a challenging hike, a long walk in the woods, or a field to run on, we&#8217;ve picked the 10 best parks around Seattle to explore.</p>

<p dir="ltr"><a href="http://www.seattle.gov/parks/find/parks/discovery-park"><strong>Discovery Park</strong></a></p>

<p dir="ltr">Discovery Parks lush green spaces and winding, easy paths are a welcome oasis in the middle of Magnolia. It&#8217;s more of a trek down stairs and trails to reach the saltwater beach, but it&#8217;s worth it for the mountain views alone. The park has a loop path that winds through the forest as well as open spaces where kids can play and look out on the Sound. The kid-friendly Environmental Learning Center also has fantastic resources those curious about the plants and animals of the park.</p>

<address dir="ltr">3801 Discovery Park Blvd, Seattle</address>

<div style='margin:5px auto; margin-bottom: 15px;' id='zone_load_1922520576'><broadstreet-zone place="0" callback="zone_load_1922520576" zone-id="89659" keywords="not_home_page,not_landing_page,is_article_page,outdoor-fun,things-to-do,parks-beaches,outdoor-fun,things-to-do,10-of-the-best-seattle-area-parks-for-kids,post" soft-keywords="true" zone-alias=""></broadstreet-zone></div><script>window.zone_load_1922520576 = function(z, d) { if (!d.count) document.getElementById('zone_load_1922520576').style.display = 'none'; };</script>

<p dir="ltr"><a href="https://www.seattle.gov/parks/find/parks/carkeek-park"><strong>Carkeek Park</strong></a></p>

<p dir="ltr">Carkeek: come for the playground, stay for the views. The salmon slide at Carkeek will occupy your kids while you admire the stunning Olympics from the shoreline. Climb up the stairs to the pedestrian overpass and take in the spectacular mountain sights while train cars pass beneath your feet.</p>

<address dir="ltr">950 NW Carkeek Park Rd, Seattle</address>

<p dir="ltr"><a href="https://www.seattle.gov/parks/find/parks/seward-park"><strong>Seward Park</strong></a></p><div class='newsletter-subscribe-area'><div class='newsletter-text'><p>For more family friendly things to do, news, events and travel, sign up for Seattle's Child newsletters</p></div><div class='newsletter-btn-area'><button type='button' class='newsletter-btn popup-newsletter popmake-14254' >Sign Up</button></div></div>

<p dir="ltr">Seward is another gem on the shores of Lake Washington. With its 300 acres of forest and green space, you might spot an eagle if youre lucky. The park is home to an <a href="http://sewardpark.audubon.org/">Audubon Center</a> where classes and camps are held year-round, including wildlife education classes and nature-focused book clubs.</p>

<address dir="ltr">5895 Lake Washington Blvd S, Seattle</address>

<p dir="ltr"><a href="https://www.seattle.gov/parks/find/parks/volunteer-park"><strong>Volunteer Park</strong></a></p>

<p dir="ltr">The largest park in Capitol Hill, Volunteer Park is a Seattle institution. In addition to a big playground and wading pool, the park boasts a rotund water tower whose 100 steps you can climb to reach the highest point in Capitol Hill. When youve reached the top, take in the gorgeous 360 view of downtown and the Eastside. Also in the park are the <a href="http://www.seattleartmuseum.org/visit/asian-art-museum">Asian Art Museum</a> and the <a href="https://volunteerparkconservatory.org/">Volunteer Park Conservatory</a> ($4 for adults, $2 for teens 13-17, free for kids under 12), which showcases an amazing array of plants and flowers.</p>

<address dir="ltr">1247 15th Ave E, Seattle</address>

<p dir="ltr"><a href="https://www.seattle.gov/parks/find/parks/green-lake-park"><strong>Green Lake</strong></a></p>

<p dir="ltr">Although its a summer standout, Green Lake also shines during the rainier months. With a 2.8 mile loop path for wheels and feet; ample green spaces to play on; a full playground; basketball, tennis, and volleyball courts; an indoor gym; and a superb wading pool in summer, Green Lakes got it all.</p>

<address dir="ltr">7201 E Greenlake Dr N, Seattle</address>

<p dir="ltr"><a href="https://www.seattle.gov/parks/find/parks/meridian-playground"><strong>Meridian Playground</strong></a></p>

<p dir="ltr">Meridian is a smaller park with a large grassy field, a playground, and a Seattle Tilth-operated P-Patch. Though its a significantly tinier park, Meridian is charming and lush, with an apple orchard amid the playfield.</p>

<address dir="ltr">4649 Sunnyside Ave N, Seattle</address>

<p dir="ltr"><a href="https://www.seattle.gov/parks/find/parks/lakeridge-park"><strong>Lakeridge Park</strong></a></p>

<p dir="ltr">Seattle Parks and Rec describes Lakeridge Park as an urban oasis of forest and running water, and thats no overstatement. Lakeridges walking trails meander alongside its Taylor Creek and provide a cool respite from the bustle of the city.</p>

<address dir="ltr">10201 Holyoke Way S, Seattle</address>

<p dir="ltr"><a href="https://www.seattle.gov/parks/find/parks/golden-gardens-park"><strong>Golden Gardens</strong></a></p>

<p dir="ltr">Though its generally understood to mean the far-west Ballard shorefront, Golden Gardens also has a park nestled next to the beach. The park has an ultra-modern playground with a cool rope structure where kids can practice climbing skills, as well as wetlands that let little ones get up close and personal with nature.</p>

<address dir="ltr">8498 Seaview Pl NW, Seattle</address>

<p dir="ltr"><a href="https://www.seattle.gov/parks/find/parks/fauntleroy-park"><strong>Fauntleroy Park</strong></a></p>

<p dir="ltr">Fauntleroy is a decidedly green space, with its woods mostly undisturbed and left in their natural state. Fauntleroys creek was made a salmon run in 1991 and has been a home for them ever since.</p>

<address dir="ltr">3951 SW Barton St, Seattle</address>


<p dir="ltr"><a href="https://parks.state.wa.us/577/Saint-Edward"><strong>St. Edward State Park </strong></a><strong>(outside Seattle but worth the trip)</strong></p>

<p dir="ltr">St. Edward State Park, hugging the banks of Lake Washington, has over 300 acres of land. It has also got arguably one of the coolest playgrounds around: an all-wood play structure with slides, turrets and tunnels. If youre looking for a laid-back day walk, youll find picnic tables and peaceful walking paths; for a more active day, the park allows boating, swimming and water-skiing. Since it&#8217;s a state park, you&#8217;ll need a <a href="http://www.discoverpass.wa.gov/133/Where-to-Buy">Discover Pass</a> for parking ($10 daily, $30 yearly). [ <a href="https://www.seattleschild.com/washington-state-parks-free-days-2021/">State parks free days in 2021</a> ]</p>

<address>14445 Juanita Dr NE, Kenmore</address>
